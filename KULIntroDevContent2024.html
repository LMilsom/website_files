Introduction to the Economics of Development

D0E32A

Luke Heath Milsom, KU Leuven, 2024/25[1] 

This document details the main concepts and readings for each week/ question. Students are expected to read the introduction to each of the main papers before the lecture (but are not expected to understand it all!). It is advised that students also read the additional reading, but this is not mandatory. This course does not have a textbook, but students are encouraged to consult the texts under general resources for background reading.

W2 Why do people stay poor?

  • Main paper(s): Balboni et al., (2022).
  • Theory: Poverty traps, and misallocation.
  • Econometrics: Identification, endogeneity, RCTs.
  • Additional reading: Barrett and Swallow (2006), Kraay and McKenzie (2014), Turnbull et al. (2024).
  • Lecture 1 slides, Lecture 2 slides.

W3 Are natural resources a solution or a trap?

  • Main paper(s): Sachs and Warner (2001), Jensen and Watchekon (2004).
  • Theory: Political economy of development, Dutch disease.
  • Econometrics: Selection on observables, fixed effects
  • Additional reading: Gollin et al. (2016), van der Ploeg (2011), Corden and Neary (1982).
  • Lecture slides.

W4 Can we manufacture prosperity?

  • Main paper(s): Rodrik (2016).
  • Theory: Structural transformation, International trade.
  • Econometrics: Fixed effects, time series
  • Additional reading: Herrendorf, Rogerson, and Valentinyi (2014), Fan et al. (2023)
  • Lecture slides.

W5 Are institutions key?

  • Main paper(s): Acemoglu et al. (2001), Glaeser et al. (2004), Albouy (2012).
  • Theory: Institutions.
  • Econometrics: Instrumental variables.
  • Additional reading: Why Nations Fail (book) Acemoglu and Robinson.
  • Lecture slides.

W6 Are credit constraints constraining development? (half, no quiz!)

  • Main paper(s): Duflo et al. (2013), Egger et al. (2023).
  • Theory: General Equilibrium effects.
  • Econometrics: RCT + IV.
  • Additional reading: Meager (2019), Banerjee et al. (2024).
  • Lecture slides.

W7 Humanities greatest invention? Is urbanisation the only route to development?

  • Main paper(s): Bryan et al. (2020), Bryan et al. (2014).
  • Theory: Urban Economics.
  • Econometrics: Inference.
  • Additional reading: Triumph of the City (Book, Glaeser), Harari (2020).
  • Lecture slides.

W8 How will climate change interact with the development challenge?

  • Main paper(s): Jedwab et al. (2023).
  • Theory: Quantitative spatial economics.
  • Econometrics: Difference in differences.
  • Additional reading: Gollin and Wolfersberger (2024).
  • Lecture slides.
